History of wireless communications

The most interesting bits will be highlighted yellow on hover

Year Description
1831 Michael Faraday predicted the existence of electromagnetic waves
1834 Morse code was invented for telegraphy
1866 Loomis patents a wireless transmission system and demonstrated it's range of at least 22 km
1880 The invention of the photophone[1], a wireless telephone that sent information using a beam of visible light, by Bell
1885 Edison patents a way of communication with electrostatic induction
1886 Heinrich Hertz identified radio waves for the first time
1888 Hertz discovered the basis of radar technology
1895 Marconi developed a telegraph system that used radio waves for communication
1897 The first wireless company was founded. It was called Wireless Telegraph and Signal Company
1899 The first international wireless message was sent by Marconi from England to France
1900 Tesla patented the first radio devices and described radar technology
1902 Ehret filed patents Frequency Modulation transmission and reception
1924 Bell Telephone Company invented the mobile telephone and fitted it in New york City police cars
1925 The first conference on radio frequency allocation was held;
