About this technology

Wireless communication is the electromagnetic transfer of information between devices. Radio waves are the most commonly used range of EM radiation for this purpose.

Wireless communication is called unguided medium because it does not require a physical medium for the signal to propagate. On the other hand, wired communication is called guided medium because it requires some sort of cable.

Benefits of wireless communication over wired communications include:

  • Mobility: you can stay connected to the wireless network in any part of the room as long as you are within range of the signal
  • Cost: there is no need to buy and install cables for a wireless network
  • Ease of installation: the effort of laying cables is absent in wireless networks
  • Reliability: no chance of cables becoming damaged since no cables are used

Disadvantages of wireless communication include:

  • Interference: if two technologies (like Bluetooth and Wi-Fi) operate on the same frequency, they can interfere with each other if used at the same time
  • Security: it is easier to intercept wireless signals than wired signals
